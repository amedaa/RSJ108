Ameda Uwaifo's Tips on Interviewing for the Socially Inept

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

How to make a totally dreadful and anxiety-inducing activity into a successful social interaction!

Assembly time: 5-60 minutes, depending on the intensity of your interview
Awesome rating: 22% awesome, 88% nauseating

Need to gather information but you're too anxious to ask? Want to make friends and get to know people better? Can't help you here. But, if the thought of grilling people for a grade or money deeply bothers your spirit, then you are in the right place! Here's how to conduct a successful interview even when every neuron in your brain is screaming at you to stop, drop, and roll.

  • Things You'll Need

  • A reliable recording device, like your voice notes app or a recorder
  • The confidence of a TikTok clout chaser
  • A set of premeditated questions
  • A bit of delusion
  • A backup notetaking device: some good, old pen and paper!
  • A deep and overwhelming fear of failure
  • A quiet location

  1. Research, research, research.

    Good and thorough research is necessary for great and effective questions. Make sure to do your research on the topic of your interview to avoid making the encounter anymore embrassing than it has to be.

  2. Prepare your questions beforehand.

    Do yourself a favor and don't flounder. Know what questions you're going to ask and save the improvization for your follow-up questions.

  3. Find a willing body!

    Whether or not its a person you bump into on the street or a specific person you have in mind, you need a secondary person for an interview. Don't be afraid of rejection; summon that false confidence and just (politely) ask!

    Don't worry! Most people are usually nice.

  4. Once you find that willing body, get out your recording devices and get to asking questions.

    Memory is a fickle thing. Have a solid record of your questions, their answers, and their consent to the interview.

  5. Don't forget to listen!

    It's easy to zone out when you're nervous and dependent on your recording devices. But LISTEN! Get out of your head and use your ears. This will be important for any follow-up questions and it makes your interviwee feel a bit more imporant.

  6. "Is there anything else you would like to add?"

    Begin wrapping up your interview, thank your interviwee and get to writing. You didnt explode! Great job!
